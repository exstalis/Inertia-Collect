This episode will explore a chapter in a book on technology - to a greater extend - that may appeal to many of us because it discusses how the latest developments in the field are influencing and guiding our shared future. Future Histories takes us back to a time when ingenuity allowed us to correlate influential historical moments.

Considering that technology is now more than just a tool, it is easy to see how it may be used as a "method" to access many facets of contemporary life. This book invites us to reflect on our stake in technology-based governance systems, even as it provides us with an overview of the changes in rights and possibilities brought about by technological progress and the implications of capital governance.

It's probably a good starting point to mention the author of Future Histories, Lizzie O'Shea. 
She is the founder and the chair of [Digital Rights Watch](https://digitalrightswatch.org.au/), which advocates for human rights online. Not only that, she is also a board member of Blueprint for Free Speech and the Alliance for Gambling Reform. She received the Davis Projects for Peace Prize for her work at the National Justice Project, where she collaborated with attorneys, journalists, and activists to launch a [Copwatch](https://www.copwatch.org.au/our-story) program. Access Now honored her a [Human Rights Hero](https://www.accessnow.org/heroes-villains-awards/) in June 2019. Please check out the links below to find more about her and her work.
