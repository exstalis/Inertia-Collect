So, let’s dive into what *flourishing* means:  
We may want to find common ground to better understand what flourishing might be. For that, let’s consider Adam Smith’s and the prevalent general view of `leftist` — that is:  
	"No society can surely be flourishing and happy, of which the far greater part of the members are poor and miserable. It is but equity, besides, that they who feed, clothe, and lodge the whole body of the people, should have such a share of the produce of their own labor as to be themselves tolerably well fed, clothed, and lodged" (A. Smith 1977, B1, Chapter 8.). 

What is tolerable should not be to the extreme point of misery.  
The majority of the world’s population is in more than miserable conditions to be counted as tolerable. We alone cannot justify this without working toward educating ourselves to have a broader perspective and practice of politics to implement forward prosperity for structural reality. We want to achieve this not for our own household but for the many lives we share our time with. Therefore, our understanding of political economy should shape and foster the economic components and values.  This will be more doable if we can understand the nature and function of the economy.  

With regard to our collective positive experience, our day-to-day social functioning, and positive psychology that enables flourishing, we are more inclined to democratize our lives and times and share our experiences together.

Flourishing is not just an individual level; nor is it solely about the accumulation of social functions in an economic context. It is a state where the individual-in-context has structural characteristics that democratize the subjective and also the individual refinement. To my understanding, subjective experience can be different than individual understanding, while we can share thoughts and emotions that are embodied in individuals with a similar experience. Subjective experience is also more granular than the individual's refined understanding of their experience.  
It is "individual-in-context."

The author, *Deva Woodly*, denotes 
	— "that this means we must think of flourishing as a proportion rather than a mean (Sechel 2019). 
	*Happier than them, but more of them are happy...* 
	and that a polity's flourishing can only be observed by taking into account what proportion of individuals are flourishing and attending to whether and what demographic and structural characteristics align with flourishing and why."


In a political framework, on the contrary, doctrines such as the American Dream or Western Liberal capitalism progress in a way to bamboozle any member of the societal structure.
